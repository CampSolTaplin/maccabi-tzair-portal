# Coordinator Portal Guide

This guide is for group coordinators. It walks you through:

1. Signing in
2. Cancelling or re-enabling a session (for a holiday, a cancelled Kinus,
   an event, or any reason)
3. Generating and printing Community Service Hours letters for your
   participants

## 1. Sign in

Open the portal:

**https://maccabi-tzair-dashboard-2-0.onrender.com**

On the sign-in screen:

- **Email or phone:** type your email address or your 10-digit US phone.
  The portal accepts any format — `(305) 555-1234`, `305-555-1234`, or
  `3055551234`.
- **Password:** if this is your first login, use the default:

  > **`M@rjcc2026`**

Click **Sign In**.

### First login: set your own password

If it's your first time, the portal will send you to **Set a new
password**. Pick a password (at least 8 characters, different from the
default), confirm it, and click **Update Password**. This is a one-time
step.

### Two-factor authentication (admins only)

If your account is marked as an Administrator, you'll also be asked for a
**6-digit code** from an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy,
etc.) after entering your password. Enter the code and check **"Trust
this device for 30 days"** so you don't have to re-enter it every time
on the same browser.

## 2. Managing sessions — cancel, restore, lock, unlock

Use this when, for example:

- A Kinus falls on a holiday and there won't be a meeting.
- You need to block a date for all groups (camp trip, machaneh).
- A madrich accidentally submitted wrong attendance and you need to let
  them fix it (that's "unlock").

### Step 1 — Open the Sessions page

In the left sidebar, click **Sessions**. You'll land on the sessions
calendar page.

### Step 2 — Filter by area (optional)

At the top you'll see four tabs:

- **All Groups**
- **Katan**
- **Noar**
- **Leadership**

Click a tab to focus on the sessions for just that area. This is useful
when you're responsible for specific groups and don't need to see others.

### Step 3 — Find the date you want to change

Sessions are grouped into two sections:

- **Upcoming Sessions** — dates in the future, shown first. This is
  usually what you want.
- **Past Sessions** — dates that have already passed, shown below in
  gray.

Inside each section, sessions are grouped by month and then by date.
Each date shows:

- The day of the week
- The day number
- Small color-coded chips for each group that has a session on that day

### Step 4 — Expand the date

Click the date row you want to act on. It expands and shows:

- An action bar with **Cancel All / Restore All / Lock All / Unlock All**
  buttons that apply to every session on that date.
- A list of each individual group session for that date, each with its
  own ban / unban and lock / unlock icons on the right side.

### Step 5 — Cancel a session

There are two ways:

#### Cancel a single session for one group

1. Click the red **Ban** icon (circle with a slash) on the row of that
   group's session.
2. A small prompt asks **"Why is this session being cancelled?"** Type
   a short reason, for example:
   - `Yom Kippur`
   - `Camp trip`
   - `Weather`
   - `Holiday`
3. Click **OK**.

The session is now marked as **CANCELLED** with your reason shown next
to it. Attendance for that session is hidden from Community Hours
calculations until it's restored.

#### Cancel every group on a given date

If the whole portal should skip a date (a Jewish holiday, for example):

1. Expand the date row.
2. Click **Cancel All** in the action bar at the top.
3. This cancels every active session under that date at once.

> **Note:** The prompt for a reason is only shown when you cancel a
> single session. "Cancel All" cancels them with a generic "Cancelled"
> label. If you want individual reasons, cancel them one by one.

### Step 6 — Restore a cancelled session

- Click the green **CheckCircle** icon on a single row, OR
- Click **Restore All** on the date to restore every cancelled session
  on that date.

The reason text is cleared and the session is active again.

### Step 7 — Lock / unlock

A **locked** session means madrichim can no longer change the attendance
for that date. Locking usually happens automatically when a madrich hits
"Lock & Submit Attendance" after filling in all the statuses.

You can also lock and unlock manually:

- **Unlock** a submitted session so a madrich can fix a mistake: click
  the amber lock icon next to the session (it turns into an open
  padlock).
- **Lock** a session to prevent further changes: click the gray open
  padlock icon.

Use **Unlock All** / **Lock All** in the date's action bar if you need
to do many at once.

### Done

There is no "Save" button — every change takes effect immediately. You
can safely close the tab.

## 3. Community Service Hours — generating letters

Use this when a participant or a parent asks for proof of their service
hours, or when the camp issues end-of-year letters.

### Step 1 — Open the Hours page

In the left sidebar, click **Community Hours**. The page title is
**Community Service Hours**.

### Step 2 — Pick the group

Below the title you'll see a row of group filter tabs: every group in
the system shown side by side. Click the group whose participants you
need letters for (for example, `Katan 5th`, `Noar 7th`, `SOM`, etc.).

The page shows:

- **Summary cards** at the top with totals:
  - How many participants are in the group
  - The hour rate per Saturday and per weekday session
  - The top performer's hours
- **A table** listing every participant with:
  - Last name, first name
  - Number of Saturday sessions attended (and total hours from them)
  - Number of weekday sessions attended
  - Event hours (special events)
  - **Total Hours** — color-coded: gray if below 50h, amber if 50–99h,
    green if 100h or more

### Step 3 — Generate a letter for ONE participant

1. Find the participant in the table.
2. Click the small **document icon** (FileText) at the far right of
   their row.
3. A new browser tab opens with the printable letter.

### Step 4 — Generate letters for the WHOLE group at once

1. Make sure you've selected the right group tab.
2. Click the **Generate All Letters** button at the top-right of the
   page.
3. A new browser tab opens with every participant's letter, one per
   page, ready to print.

### Step 5 — Print the letters

Once the new tab is open with the letters:

1. Press **Cmd + P** (Mac) or **Ctrl + P** (Windows/Linux) to open the
   print dialog.
2. In the destination, pick your printer.
3. Paper size: **Letter (8.5 × 11 in)**.
4. Make sure **Background graphics** is enabled — on Chrome it's under
   "More settings", on Safari it's "Print backgrounds". This keeps the
   Maccabi Tzair header colors and logo on the printed page.
5. Click **Print**.

> **To save as PDF instead of printing on paper:** in the print dialog,
> choose **"Save as PDF"** as the destination. You'll get one PDF file
> with every letter inside it. You can then email it or upload it
> anywhere.

### Tips

- If a participant doesn't have any sessions yet, they will still appear
  in the list but with 0 hours. You can still generate their letter, it
  just won't have much to show.
- The hours are recalculated every time you open the page. So if you
  cancel a session (see section 2), the hours for everyone who would
  have attended that session drop accordingly.
- Event hours are added manually by the admin. If you think a
  participant's event hours are wrong, contact the admin.

## 4. Signing out

Click your name or avatar on the top-right and choose **Sign Out**.

## Common questions

**I clicked Cancel All by mistake.**
No problem — click the date to expand it again, and click **Restore
All**. The sessions come back immediately, as if it never happened.

**The participant I need is not in the Community Hours list.**
They're probably in a different group. Check the group filter tabs at
the top of the Hours page. If you still can't find them, they might not
be registered as a participant in any group — contact the admin.

**The letter is missing the Maccabi Tzair logo when I print.**
Your browser is stripping background graphics. In the print dialog,
look for "Background graphics" or "Print backgrounds" and turn it on.

**Madrichim are telling me they can't submit attendance.**
Check that the session is not locked. Open the Sessions page, find the
date, expand it, and if the group's session shows a yellow padlock, click
it to unlock. The madrich can then submit again.

**Can I cancel a past session?**
Yes, but it's unusual. You might do it when fixing historical data — for
example, you realized a specific date should never have counted because
of an event. Cancelling a past session will remove those hours from the
Community Hours report.
